Passion Plus Disability Services offer a comprehensive range of services tailored to meet the diverse needs of our clients.

COMING ONBOARD

Our process begins with a referral from the participant themselves, a Local Area Coordinator (LAC) or a support coordinator

We will organise a meeting with the participant, their family, or other support networks to gather information about the participant's needs, goals, and current situation. This may include a review and understanding of any existing NDIS plan.

This Involves:

Assessment of Client's Need: We ensure we carry out a detailed assessment of the participant's needs, including their, goals, functional capacity, and desired outcomes. Goal Setting: Participants work with Passion Plus to identify and set specific goals. Support Plan Development: From the outcome of our assessment and goal setting, a support plan is developed that outlines the specific supports and services required. This is a person-centered Support Plan, tailored to the individual's unique needs, choices and preferences.

This Involves:

  • Service Agreements: Providers will create service agreements that clearly outline the services to be provided, costs, and responsibilities of both parties.
  • Service Delivery: Participants begin receiving the supports and services outlined in their plan.
  • Regular Monitoring and Review: The support plan is regularly reviewed and updated to ensure it remains effective and meets the participant's changing needs.

  • Open Communication: Providers should maintain open communication with participants, their families, and other support networks.
  • Capacity Building: The onboarding process should also focus on building the participant's capacity to manage their own supports and make informed decisions.
  • Feedback and Complaints: Participants should have access to clear feedback and complaints mechanisms.

  • Person-centered approach: The process should be tailored to the individual's unique needs and preferences, putting them at the center of decision-making.
  • Clear communication: Participants should understand the process, their rights, and how to access support.
  • Building trust and rapport: Providers should build strong relationships with participants based on trust and respect.
  • Focus on participant choice and control: Participants should have choice and control over their supports and services.
  • Ensuring safety and well-being: The process should prioritize the safety and well-being of participants at all times.
